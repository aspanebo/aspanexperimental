Q1. What services do you specialize in?

I work across creative production and visual media — including video editing, motion design, content production, creative direction, and experimental visuals. I combine artistic and cinematic approaches to create meaningful visual stories.

Q2. Do you work with international clients?

Yes — I collaborate with clients and creative teams worldwide. Communication is available in English and Russian, and projects can be managed remotely.

Q3. What types of projects do you usually work on?

I work on commercial videos, social media content, creative campaigns, independent projects, artistic collaborations, and portfolio-driven film/media concepts.

Q4. How do we start working together?

Send your project idea or brief via the Contact page. Please include: goals, references, deadlines, and your budget range. After that, I'll review the concept and suggest the best direction.

Q5. Do you work with fixed pricing or hourly rates?

Both options are possible. Project-based pricing is used for clear deliverables, while hourly or retainer format works for ongoing collaboration.

Q6. Can you help shape the creative idea for the project?

Yes — I can assist with concept development, moodboards, visual style, and storytelling direction before production begins.

Q7. Do you participate in collaborations and indie projects?

Yes — I'm open to meaningful creative collaborations, festival projects, and artistic initiatives, especially those focused on visual storytelling and experimentation.

Q8. What is your typical turnaround time?

Timing depends on project complexity. Short-form content may take a few days, while full creative or cinematic projects require longer timelines.

Q9. Do you offer revisions?

Yes — every project includes a defined number of revision rounds to refine visuals and align with the final vision.

Q10. Can you work on long-term or partnership-based projects?

Absolutely — I'm open to ongoing collaboration, creative teams, startup projects, and long-term production workflows.
